# Appendix — F0 Reconstruction: The Three Walls

**Status.** Companion to the frozen core. This appendix strengthens the logical
basis of **F0** without altering any frozen statement of F0, F0-L, C1–C5, or
P1–P7. It classifies F0's own claims by evidential tier and declares the
premises its non-theorem walls rest on. Where it adds to the payment taxonomy it
does so by *extension* (P8, P9), not revision. It consolidates two things the
frozen core already states — the borrowed limitative results (F0's genealogy)
and F0's self-application — and sharpens them; it introduces no new frozen
commitment.

---

## 0. The objection this answers

In the frozen core, F0 is set out as a **pattern table**: each explanatory system
occupies a row showing what it *secures* (a subset of C1–C5) and what it *pays*
(a P-move). The objection: the verdict in each cell — "this system pays P-k" —
is adjudicated *inside* F0's own framework. A defender of any system can reply,
"I pay nothing; you have merely redescribed my position as a payment." So the
table looks immune to counterexample, hence circular.

The reconstruction makes explicit the **three external walls** that the frozen
genealogy already names — Agrippa (C5), Hume (C3), Gödel/Tarski (C1 ∧ C2) — and
on which the table's regularity rests. These results exist independently of F0
and, in two of three cases, are acknowledged by F0's own opponents; F0 does not
*set* the walls, it *reads them off*. The table remains F0's inductive face (as
frozen); the appendix adds that its regularity is **backed** by walls external to
F0, so no disputed cell can move the impossibility — the walls carry it, the
cells display it.

---

## 1. The three walls

The five conditions partition cleanly across three external results.

### Wall 1 — Gödel (Theorem Tier). Blocks C1 ∧ C2.

This is F0-L. C1 ("E finally and consistently explains W") entails completeness
(D), consistency (A), and — because W contains arithmetic — expressiveness (C).
C2 ("S is identifiable within E and survives as the bearer of E") entails
self-inclusion (B). Hence:

> **C1 ∧ C2 ⟹ A ∧ B ∧ C ∧ D.**

By Gödel's first incompleteness theorem, A ∧ B ∧ C ∧ D cannot jointly hold.
Therefore C1 ∧ C2 cannot jointly hold. This is a **theorem**, resting only on
the near-trivial premise that W contains arithmetic structure.

### Wall 2 — Agrippa (Strong-Argument Tier). Blocks C5, hence C3.

C5 requires the ground of V to be "given without circularity or leap." Any
justificatory chain is, by the Münchhausen/Agrippa trilemma, structurally one
of: infinite regress, circle, or an underived terminus. C5's own wording forbids
the circle ("without circularity") and the underived terminus ("without leap"),
and its demand that the ground be *given* (finitely arrived at) forbids the
regress. So C5 requires escaping all three horns — which the trilemma says is
impossible.

C3 ("objective binding force") depends on C5: binding that is not grounded is
attitude-relative, not objective. Hence C3 rides on Wall 2. (A system may secure
*intersubjective* binding without C5 — but that weakens C3's "objective" to
"intersubjective," which is the P3 payment, not an escape.)

This wall rests on a **declared, contestable premise** — see §6.

### Wall 3 — No guaranteed desert (Empirical / Descriptive Tier). Blocks C4.

C4 requires that actions and consequences (A→R) be "recovered (repaid /
reconciled) along V." No law of the natural order guarantees that desert is met:
the virtuous are not reliably repaid. Securing C4 therefore requires positing an
unverifiable settling mechanism (providence, afterlife, karmic ledger) — the P6
payment. This is not a theorem but a robust near-empirical fact about the
natural order. (Stated neutrally to avoid the theistic framing of the "problem
of evil.")

## 2. Why the walls defuse the circularity charge

The adjudication a wall requires is **structural, not evaluative**.

- **Wall 1** asks: does the system retain A ∧ B ∧ C while claiming D? — a formal
  question.
- **Wall 2** asks: does the justification graph terminate, loop, or regress? —
  a topological question about the system's own commitments.
- **Wall 3** asks: does the system posit a settling mechanism beyond natural
  law? — a question about its posits.

Crucially, Wall 2 does **not** require F0 to call any stop "dogmatic." That
value-word was the whole source of the residual circularity. A foundationalist
who says "my basic beliefs need no justification" has not escaped the trilemma;
he has explicitly chosen the third horn (an underived terminus). Whether he
honorifically calls it "properly basic" or pejoratively "dogmatic" is irrelevant
to F0: C5 requires the ground of V to be "given without circularity or leap,"
and an underived terminus is precisely a leap, so C5 is not met.
He may accept this ("and that is fine") — but that is *accepting the payment*,
not avoiding it.

The one thing this does **not** dissolve is named and owned in §6: whether an
*intrinsically self-justifying* terminus is possible at all. That is a real,
declared premise, not a hidden verdict.

## 3. Headline vs. sorting: what is theorem-grade and what is not

A single inference fixes the evidential status of F0's central claim. Because
any system securing all five must in particular secure C1 ∧ C2, and C1 ∧ C2 is
impossible (Wall 1, theorem), **the existence thesis — "no system secures all
five" — is entailed by Wall 1 alone.** It does not depend on Walls 2 or 3. Given
W ⊇ arithmetic, it is a **theorem**.

Two honest consequences:

- **The headline is cheap.** "You cannot have all five" is essentially Gödel
  translated into the vocabulary of C1–C5. Not a deep new discovery; a rigorous
  but modest restatement.
- **F0's real content — and real risk — is the *sorting*.** The substantive,
  non-trivial work is showing that systems which *decline* C1 ∧ C2 still cannot
  obtain C3, C4, C5 for free, and locating each system's specific payment. That
  work rests on Walls 2 (Strong-Argument) and 3 (Descriptive), and on the
  empirical program downstream. The theory's originality lives here, not in the
  headline.

This separation is a defensive asset. The downstream empirical wager (the
critical-transition program) and the contested premise of Wall 2 can both be
challenged **without touching the headline**, which is theorem-secured.
Conversely, the theory must not advertise the sorting with the confidence of the
headline.

## 4. The table is a display board

Given §2–§3, the table's evidential role — the frozen displayed regularity that
upgrades F0 from assertion toward demonstration — is seen to *rest on* the walls.
A correction to any single cell (a better reading of Kant or Hegel) does not move
the walls; the impossibility is upstream of the cells. This is the precise
mechanism behind the frozen note that individual cells are interpretive and
revisable while the pattern stands: the cells display, the walls decide.

## 5. Completeness of the P-moves over Wall 1's exits

F0-L names four exits from the C1 ∧ C2 impossibility — drop one of A, B, C, D.
Mapping the P-moves onto these exits shows the taxonomy is a **grid filling**,
not an arbitrary list, and exposes two exits that were previously unnumbered.

| Exit from Wall 1 | Effect | P-move |
|---|---|---|
| drop D (completeness) | refuse to complete: silence / dissolve the question | P2, P7 |
| drop B (self-inclusion) | place the system outside its own scope | P1 (external warrant), P5 (two-layering) |
| drop A (consistency) | tolerate contradiction | **P8 (new)** |
| drop C (expressiveness) | retreat to a self-blind language | **P9 (new)** |

Two additions close the grid:

- **P8 — Abandonment of consistency.** A dialetheist who tolerates contradiction
  can claim completeness with self-inclusion, appearing to breach Wall 1. F0
  replies: consistency (A) has been paid. Without P8 the table has no cell in
  which to record this; with it, Wall 1's four exits are fully covered.
- **P9 — Self-blindness.** A system may secure completeness and consistency with
  self-inclusion by retreating to a language too weak to talk about itself
  (Presburger arithmetic is complete and consistent precisely because it lacks
  the expressive power for Gödelian self-reference). This is distinct from P1:
  P1 places the ground at an *external privileged point* (a God's-eye
  standpoint); P9 keeps the standpoint internal but *expressively impoverished*
  so self-reference never arises. Distinguishing them keeps the taxonomy clean.

**A note on P2 and P7.** These are meta-refusals, not object-level exits: P2
declines to assert; P7 declares a demand meaningless. P7 is aimed less at Wall
1's completeness (D) than at Wall 2's demand for a ground — it dissolves the
C5/C3 question. But it reaches no *outside*: the meaning-criterion by which it
voids ground-questions is itself an ungrounded ground-level claim — an underived
terminus at Wall 2, or self-refuting. Likewise the more radical reading —
rejecting the whole C1–C5 demand structure — is not a counterexample: declining
to seek the conditions is the limiting case of payment (secure nothing), not an
escape from the walls.

Outside Wall 1: C2-thinning is P4; the C3/C5 concession (Wall 2) is P3; the C4
concession (Wall 3) is P6.

## 6. Declared premises and dependencies (honesty ledger)

F0 states, rather than hides, the commitments its non-theorem walls rest on.

- **Wall 2 premise.** The framework assumes there is no *intrinsically
  self-justifying terminus* (no fourth option beyond the trilemma's three
  horns). This is an unprovable substantive epistemological claim; a
  foundationalist may contest it. If contested successfully, the system in
  question is classified as having *declined* C5 ("ground without leap"), not as
  a counterexample to F0. Declared premise, not hidden verdict.
- **Wall 3 status.** Descriptive / near-empirical, not a theorem. Defeasible in
  principle by a demonstrated natural law guaranteeing desert; none is known.
- **C4 attribution → C2 (F12 dependency).** C4 splits into *attribution* (A→R is
  imputable to S) and *reconciliation* (repaid along V). Reconciliation rests on
  Wall 3 (robust). Attribution rests on the free-will question, where a
  compatibilist can claim proximate authorship without payment. F0 therefore
  rests C4's weight on reconciliation and routes attribution to C2
  (subject-thinning, P4). Whether attribution is cleanly absorbed there depends
  on the refinement of **F12** (self-as-mode); this dependency is declared, not
  assumed away.
- **Tier discipline.** Only Wall 1 is a theorem. Conflating the tiers would
  restore the overclaim the circularity charge fed on. Tiers: Wall 1 =
  Theorem; Wall 2 = Strong Argument; Wall 3 = Descriptive Fact.

## 7. F0's own row

F0 applies its schema to itself. Its universal form ("no *possible* system
secures all five") would demand C5 — a final ground for the universal claim. F0
declines that demand; it halts at a fallible, falsifiable pattern. So F0
occupies its own table:

> **F0** — *Secures:* C1 ∧ C2 (via F0-L, proven) plus, inductively, the sorting.
> *Pays:* **P2** at Wall 2 — it declines the final ground of its universal form
> and halts at a corrigible pattern.

F0 is thus not an exception seated outside the table. It is the one row that
states its own payment in advance. Its claim is not "F0 escapes the trade-off"
but "F0 makes the trade-off visible and chooses its payment consciously."

---

## 8. Falsification, restated

The frozen condition — "add a row that secures all five with an empty 'Pays',
without dropping a demand" — sharpens into three simultaneous tasks, each set by
an external wall (so the goalposts are not F0's to move):

Exhibit a **single** system that at once —

1. is complete, consistent, self-including, and expressive — **breaching Wall 1
   (Gödel);**
2. grounds V without regress, circle, or underived terminus — **breaching Wall 2
   (Agrippa);**
3. guarantees, by natural law and without a posited settling mechanism, that
   A→R is reconciled along V — **breaching Wall 3 (desert).**

Task 1 is impossible by theorem; tasks 2 and 3 run against the strongest
available external results. The condition stays genuinely falsifiable — the pass
criteria are explicit — while making clear why no such system is in view.

---

*This appendix changes no frozen statement. It supplies F0's external basis (the
three walls), fixes the evidential tier of each claim (theorem / strong argument
/ descriptive), extends the payment taxonomy to close Wall 1's exits (P8, P9),
and declares the premises and dependencies on which the non-theorem walls rest.*
